Challenges
As a technical consultant at Appian, I worked with clients to solve complex business problems and deliver high-quality technical solutions. However, there were several challenges that we faced in the process:
  1. UX/UI design: Many clients prioritize functionality over design, but good design is essential for adoption and productivity. Some of the challenges we faced around UX/UI design included complex workflows, low user engagement, and inconsistent design. For example, one of our clients had a complex business process that required multiple steps to complete. The original design had several screens and buttons, which made the process confusing and frustrating for users. We addressed this by creating a simple and intuitive interface that guided users through the workflow with clear instructions and visual cues.
  2. Vague requirements: In software development, requirements can be vague or unclear, making it difficult to deliver a high-quality product. To mitigate this, we made sure to have clear and frequent communication with the client. We used prototyping to help clarify requirements and used feedback loops to ensure that we were on the right track. We also involved the client in sprint demos to ensure that we were meeting their expectations.
  3. Time constraints: Delivering projects on time and within budget can be a challenge, especially in an Agile environment. To manage this, we ensured that we had a clear understanding of the requirements and prioritized the most critical features. We also ensured that we adhered to the sprint timelines and made adjustments when necessary.
  4. Technical debt: As with any software development project, technical debt can accumulate over time and make it difficult to maintain and scale the codebase. To address this, we incorporated continuous integration and delivery processes into our development cycle. We also conducted regular code reviews and made sure that our code adhered to best practices and industry standards.
